Stocks represent ownership in a company. Shareholders benefit from price appreciation and dividends.

Bonds are debt instruments issued by governments or companies. Investors earn fixed interest and receive principal later.

Mutual Funds pool money from many investors and invest across diversified assets.

ETFs are exchange-traded funds that track indices or sectors and trade like stocks.

Equity refers to ownership value after liabilities are deducted.

Dividends are profit distributions paid to shareholders.

Market Capitalization equals share price multiplied by total shares outstanding.

Portfolio means a collection of financial investments.

Diversification reduces risk by spreading investments.

Inflation decreases purchasing power over time.

Interest Rate is the cost of borrowing or reward for saving.

Liquidity measures how quickly assets convert to cash.

Assets include cash, property, stocks, and investments owned.

Liabilities are loans or obligations that must be repaid.

Net worth is calculated as assets minus liabilities.

Bull markets show rising prices and investor confidence.

Bear markets reflect falling prices and negative sentiment.

Blue-chip companies are financially strong market leaders.

Growth stocks focus on expansion rather than dividends.

Value stocks trade below estimated intrinsic value.

ROI measures profit earned relative to investment cost.

EPS shows company profit per outstanding share.

P/E ratio compares stock price with earnings.

Treasury bills are short-term government securities.

Fixed deposits provide guaranteed bank returns.

Volatility measures market price swings.

Financial literacy improves money decision-making.
